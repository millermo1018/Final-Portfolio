Food

Chicago is known for being a top rated city to eat and drink in. The city has even won awards for our tasty food, such as being rated the food and drink capital of the world according to a global survey via "Time Out Index". Here are a few of my favorite places to eat in the city!

Batter & Berries

For Breakfast, I enjoy going to Batter and Berries to indulge in their world famous french toast! Batter & Berries resides in the Lincoln Park neighborhood of the city with an address of 2748 N Lincoln Ave, Chicago, IL 60614. The doors to Batter & Berries opened up back in 2012 by Dr. Tanya & Craig Richardson who had the passion to add an eclectic world class restaurant to brunch scene in the city.The popular breakfast spot has been serving up creative offerings, such as decadent French toast flights and deconstructed omelets, and so much more. While Batter & Berries is most known for their take on breakfast food items, the restaurant also serves Lunch and Dinner.


Sip and Savor

If you’re looking to satisfy your caffeine cravings or need a cozy work environment, head over to Sip & Savor. With multiple locations in the city, coffee lovers have the option to visit the Bronzeville, Hyde Park, or Rosenwald location. This coffee house, established in 2005 by Trez V. Pugh III Chicagoans from all walks of life who stop in for a break, a good cup of coffee and great community-focused conversations. Known for its exceptional quality and flavor, Sip & Savor coffees are certified fair trade, and in some cases, certified organic and shade grown. Be sure to stop by and have a sip! .


Can't Believe it's not Meat


To all those with a plant based diet or just want to switch up their palette, head on over to “Can't Believe It’s Not Meat” in the Hyde Park neighborhood. The focus of “Can't Believe It’s Not Meat” is a modern, flavorful, soulful To-Go Vegetarian and Vegan restaurant that you will be sure to love!


Dat Donut


To satisfy your sweet tooth craving, go on over to my favorite Donut shop known as “Dat Donut”’. Dat Donut has been a Chatham neighborhood staple since 1994 and they are most known for their infamous “Big Dat” donut. The owners, Darryl and Andrea, opened their flagship location on the corner of 83rd Street and Cottage Grove Avenue. The team at Dat Donut takes pride in a good quality product and friendly service and their bakers have combined experience exceeding 25 years specializing in fresh and delicious hand-cut donuts.
